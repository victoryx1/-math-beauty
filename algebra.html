From the impulse to count to the symphony of abstract structures — humanity's six-thousand-year journey of taming the unknown with symbols.

发展历史

Historical Development

约公元前 4000 年  c. 4000 BCE

计数与记数系统 Numeration Systems

苏美尔人、古埃及人发明了最早的记数系统。苏美尔人使用六十进制（至今留存在时间与角度中），埃及人使用十进制。数字从具体物件中解放出来，成为独立的抽象实体。
The Sumerians used base-60 (still surviving in our time and angle measurements), while the Egyptians used base-10. Numbers were liberated from physical objects, becoming independent abstract entities.

约公元前 1800 年  c. 1800 BCE

巴比伦泥板：二次方程求解 Babylonian Quadratic Equations

巴比伦人掌握了二次方程的数值解法，在泥板上刻下了 √2 的近似值（精确到六位十进制）。普林顿 322 号泥板记录了毕达哥拉斯三元组，表明他们已掌握深刻的数论知识。
The Babylonians mastered numerical solutions to quadratic equations. The Plimpton 322 tablet records Pythagorean triples, revealing profound number-theoretic knowledge.

约公元 820 年  c. 820 CE

花剌子米与代数学的诞生 Al-Khwārizmī & the Birth of Algebra

波斯数学家花剌子米撰写《代数学》（Kitāb al-Jabr wa-l-Muqābala），首次给出二次方程的一般解法。"代数"（Algebra）源自 al-Jabr，"算法"（Algorithm）源自他的名字。
The Persian mathematician wrote "Kitāb al-Jabr", giving the first general method for solving quadratic equations. "Algebra" derives from al-Jabr; "Algorithm" from his name al-Khwārizmī.

约 1100 年  c. 1100 CE

奥马尔·海亚姆 Omar Khayyám

波斯诗人兼数学家利用圆锥曲线（圆、抛物线、双曲线）的交点对三次方程进行几何分类与求解——比笛卡尔早五百余年。
The Persian poet-mathematician used intersections of conic sections to geometrically classify and solve cubic equations — five centuries before Descartes.

1545 年  1545 CE

《大术》：三、四次方程求根公式 Ars Magna: Cubic & Quartic Formulas

卡尔达诺公布三次方程求根公式（来自塔尔塔利亚），费拉里给出四次方程解法。为解三次方程，复数被正式引入数学。
Cardano published the cubic formula (from Tartaglia); Ferrari solved the quartic. Complex numbers were formally introduced to handle the "irreducible case" of cubics.

1829 — 1832 年  1829–1832

伽罗瓦：群论的革命 Galois: The Group Theory Revolution

21岁的伽罗瓦在决斗前夜写下遗稿，用"群"的概念彻底证明了五次及以上方程无一般求根公式。他开创的群论成为贯穿现代数学与理论物理的通用语言。
The 21-year-old Galois, the night before his fatal duel, scribbled down the theory that proved quintics and beyond have no general radical solution. Group theory became the universal language of modern mathematics and physics.

19世纪末 — 20世纪初  Late 19th – Early 20th C.

抽象代数的兴起 Rise of Abstract Algebra

戴德金、希尔伯特、诺特（Emmy Noether）将代数从数的运算抽象为结构的学问——环、域、理想、模不再依附于具体的数，而服从纯粹的公理。诺特被爱因斯坦称为"有史以来最伟大的女数学家"。
Dedekind, Hilbert, and Noether abstracted algebra from number operations to the study of structures — rings, fields, ideals, modules. Einstein called Noether "the most significant creative mathematical genius thus far produced."

1945 年以后  1945–present

范畴论与代数的新统一 Category Theory & New Unification

艾伦伯格与麦克莱恩引入范畴论，将群、环、拓扑空间等纳入统一的框架。代数不再是关于"数"的学问，而是关于"结构之间的关系"的学问。
Eilenberg and Mac Lane introduced category theory, unifying groups, rings, and topological spaces in one framework. Algebra became the study of relationships between structures, not just numbers.

重要结论

Key Theorems

代数基本定理 Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

$$a_n z^n + \cdots + a_0 = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; n \text{ complex roots}$$

每一个 n 次复系数方程在复数域中恰有 n 个根（计重数）。高斯在 1799 年的博士论文中首次给出了近乎严格的证明。复数域是"代数封闭"的。
Every degree-n complex polynomial has exactly n complex roots (counting multiplicity). Gauss gave the first near-rigorous proof in his 1799 doctoral dissertation.

阿贝尔-鲁菲尼定理 Abel–Ruffini Theorem

$$\text{Quintics and beyond: no general radical formula}$$

1824年阿贝尔证明五次方程无一般根式解。伽罗瓦随后用群论给出任意方程有根式解的充要条件——方程可解等价于其伽罗瓦群为可解群。
Abel proved in 1824 that the general quintic is unsolvable by radicals. Galois then gave the necessary and sufficient condition: a polynomial is solvable iff its Galois group is solvable.

二次方程求根公式 Quadratic Formula

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$$

从花剌子米的配方法中脱胎而出。判别式 Δ=b²−4ac 揭示了全部秘密：正则二实根、零则重根、负则二共轭复根。
Born from al-Khwārizmī's method of completing the square. The discriminant reveals all: positive → two real roots; zero → double root; negative → two conjugate complex roots.

卡尔达诺公式 Cardano's Cubic Formula

$$x = \sqrt[3]{-\frac{q}{2}+\sqrt{\frac{q^2}{4}+\frac{p^3}{27}}} + \sqrt[3]{-\frac{q}{2}-\sqrt{\frac{q^2}{4}+\frac{p^3}{27}}}$$

16世纪意大利数学家们的智力角斗催生了此公式。有趣的是，即使三个根都是实数，中间量也可能是复数——"不可约情形"促成了复数的全面接受。
Born from the intellectual duels of 16th-century Italian mathematicians. The "casus irreducibilis" — where all roots are real yet intermediate terms are complex — forced the full acceptance of complex numbers.
